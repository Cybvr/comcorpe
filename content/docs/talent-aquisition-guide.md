# Acquisition Campaign

## Building the Comcorpᵉ Capability Network

Recruitment Campaign 2026

## Why We Are Doing This

Comcorpᵉ helps organisations solve complex growth challenges.

When a challenge comes in, we don't look for available people. We assemble a StrikeTeam with the exact expertise required to solve the problem.

To do that consistently, we need a strong network of trusted specialists.

This campaign is about building that network.

## What We Are Building

We are building a Capability Network: a curated community of specialists across disciplines including:

- Growth Strategy
- Customer Research
- Behavioural Science
- Data Analysis
- Design
- AI
- Facilitation
- Industry Expertise

When opportunities arise, specialists from the network can be assembled into StrikeTeams based on the challenge at hand.

## Step 1: Build the Target List

We identify specialists we believe could add value to the network.

These may come from:

- Referrals
- Professional communities
- Previous engagements
- Universities
- Industry networks
- Trusted recommendations

The goal is simple: find people we would confidently deploy on a client challenge.

## Step 2: Reach Out

Once we identify someone, we introduce Comcorpᵉ and start a conversation.

We explain:

- Who we are
- What we are building
- How the StrikeTeam model works
- Why we selected them

Then we ask:

> "Would you be open to exploring future collaboration opportunities with Comcorpᵉ?"

## Step 3: Have a Conversation

If they're interested, we schedule a call.

This is an opportunity for both sides to learn more about each other.

We want to understand:

- What they do best
- The challenges they enjoy solving
- Their experience and expertise
- The type of work they want to be involved in

At the same time, we explain:

- The Comcorpᵉ vision
- The Capability Network
- The type of opportunities we expect to create
- What participation in the network looks like

By the end of the conversation, both sides should know whether there is a strong fit.

## Step 4: Align on Expectations

If there is mutual interest, we discuss how we would work together.

This includes:

- Availability
- Rates
- Ways of working
- Payment terms
- Confidentiality
- Intellectual property

The goal is to ensure expectations are clear before moving forward.

## Step 5: Complete Registration

Candidates who wish to proceed are invited to complete the Comcorpᵉ Specialist Registration Form.

The form helps us capture:

- Professional background
- Areas of expertise
- Industry experience
- Portfolio links
- Availability
- Commercial preferences

This becomes their profile within the Capability Network.

## Step 6: Review and Approve

Once the registration is complete, we review the information provided.

We assess:

- Relevance of expertise
- Quality of experience
- Evidence of past delivery
- Alignment with the type of work we undertake

Our goal is not to build the biggest network.

Our goal is to build a trusted network.

## Step 7: Welcome to the Network

Approved specialists are formally admitted into the Comcorpᵉ Capability Network.

They become part of a curated ecosystem of experts who may be invited into future StrikeTeams.

This marks the beginning of an ongoing relationship with Comcorpᵉ.

## Step 8: Assemble StrikeTeams

When a client challenge arrives, we identify the capabilities required to solve it.

We then assemble a StrikeTeam from the Capability Network.

Specialists are selected based on expertise, experience, and suitability for the challenge.

The right people come together for the right mission.

## What Every Specialist Should Leave With

You are not joining a recruitment database.

You are joining a curated network of specialists assembled to solve meaningful growth challenges.

When opportunities emerge that match your expertise, you may be invited to join a StrikeTeam built specifically for that mission.

Talent brings expertise.

Comcorpᵉ owns the outcome.

## How to Keep the Network Warm

### 1. Monthly Network Briefing

A short monthly email, not a meeting by default. It should give them useful Comcorpᵉ thinking: market signals, sectors being watched, anonymised challenge themes, and where Comcorpᵉ sees growth opportunities emerging.

The value to them: they get sharp market intelligence and stay close to the company without giving up time.

### 2. Quarterly Insider Session

An optional 45-minute session every quarter. This should feel like an insider briefing, not a townhall. Share what Comcorpᵉ is learning, what kinds of client problems are emerging, and how the Capability Network is being used.

The value to them: they understand where future work may come from and how Comcorpᵉ thinks.

### 3. Curated Peer Introductions

Make occasional one-to-one introductions where there is a strong reason. For example, complementary expertise, shared sector focus, or possible future StrikeTeam fit.

The value to them: useful professional relationships without forced networking.

### 4. Paid Advisory Calls

Invite selected specialists to paid one-hour advisory calls when Comcorpᵉ needs expert input on a sector, client problem, or opportunity area.

The value to them: they are paid for their expertise, and Comcorpᵉ learns how they think.

### 5. Paid Micro-Reviews

Ask selected specialists to review a brief, framework, hypothesis, market note, or client challenge for a fee. Keep it tightly scoped and low-effort.

The value to them: paid, contained work that can lead to deeper involvement.

### 6. Specialist Spotlights

Occasionally feature a specialist in the monthly briefing. Keep it short: what they do, what they are strong at, and what kinds of challenges they are suited for.

The value to them: visibility inside a curated professional network.

### 7. Annual Network Briefing

Run one annual session to share what Comcorpᵉ has built, what is coming next, and how the Capability Network fits into the long-term model.

The value to them: they see the bigger opportunity and feel part of something with direction.
